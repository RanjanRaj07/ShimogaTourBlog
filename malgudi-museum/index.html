Malgudi Days Museum

Fictional Town Malgudi Is Now A Reality In Karnataka

About

Anyone who grew up in the 1980s and 1990s may identify with or comprehend the significance of Malgudi Days. Those were the days when every child's and even elder's evening consisted of running home from the playground upon hearing the famous song "tanaa na taana naa." The 1980s hit TV show "Malgudi Days" was based on R K Narayan's book of the same name. The book, which featured short stories from a Karnataka village centered around Swami, the young protagonist, and his pals, was first published in 1943. A youngster next door's incredibly relatable stories warmed millions of hearts. Malgudi was a made-up South Indian village.

The book's short stories and simple narratives were filmed in Agumbe and nearby villages in the Karnataka region of Shimoga, capturing the feel of a traditional village. The series was filmed in the famous Arasalu railway station, which is featured in numerous episodes. Around and inside this station is where the village scene is set. The Mysuru Railway division chose to rehabilitate and transform the station into a Malgudi Museum including stories and excerpts from the television series and book as a tribute to the renowned writer R K Narayanan and the show.

The Malgudi Museum was designed and built between 2019 and 2020. The majority of the series was filmed at the Arasalu railway station in Karnataka. By converting the station into a museum, Mysuru Division honors the station. The dilapidated and abandoned railway station structure is currently being renovated and revitalized to preserve the recollections of the past. Even after being broadcast on Doordarshan for so long, Malgudi Days continues to evoke strong feelings. Malgudi Days still excites everyone who has watched or worked on the series, as well as the residents of Agumbe and Arasalu.

The Shimoga district headquarters is only 34 kilometers from the Arasalu train station. Beautiful scenery and settings were used to develop and film the series' fictional town of Agumbe. Particularly after the rain, Agumbe and the Ghat region have some of the most picturesque vistas and scenery. Many stories and incidents are depicted on the walls of a little station on the Shivamogga-Talaguppa railway line that has been transformed into the Malgudi Museum. Upon entering the station, you will pass through a charming little garden and witness the famous Malgudi Chai café. The café has the vibe of a country café from the show because it is shaped like a purple train coach with two benches outside of it. The museum is filled with a variety of items that seem to be taken directly out of R. K. Narayana's stories, including pictures, artifacts, manuscripts, and vessels. When you go into the museum, the background music from Malgudi Days plays, transporting you to the time and place of the Malgudi Days. For all of the Malgudi and R K Narayanan enthusiasts, this British-era railway station is quite surprising.

Arasalu railway station or Malgudi Museum is just 34 km away from district headquarters Shivamogga. The museum can be well reached by road.

Auto rickshaws or cabs are easily available from the railway station to Shilpa Kala Village.The comfortable and scenic roads from Shimoga lead to Arasalu or Malgudi Museum in just 30-40 minutes by road. The station is close to 34 km away from Shimoga. You can either hire a cab or take public transport.
  • By Air:
    • The nearest international and domestic airport is Mangalore International Airport about 200 km away. Bengaluru is about 300 km and takes approximately 6-7 hours to reach Shimoga.
  • By Road:
    • If you want to get the feel of the most iconic railway station board a train that reaches Arasalu. Shimoga –Talaguppa passenger train which starts at 2.40 pm from Shimoga takes about 45 minutes to reach Arasalu. The railway track is upgraded to a broad gauge from a meter gauge.

Museum Timings and Fees:

The museum is open on all days from 10 am to 6 pm except Tuesday. The entry fee to the Museum is Rs 5 per person above the age of 5 years. There is no need to reserve or buy in advance. You can buy the tickets at the location when visiting.

Places to visit in and around:

The scenic and beautiful Agumbe Ghat section invites a lot of adventure enthusiasts, bird-watchers, photographers, and nature lovers. The other places of interest are Kavaledurga Fort ( 55 km), Jog Falls ( 71 Km), Nagara Fort ( 50 km), Sakrebyle Elephant Camp ( 50 km), Keladi Rameshwara Temple ( 48 km), Ikkeri Temple ( 44 km) and Honnemaradu ( 68 km).
